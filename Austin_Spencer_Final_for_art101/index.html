Revelation

The Veil Licks Itself to Sleep

We came upon the salt veil where the tetractys was knotted,
where silence fed on itself and left only the dry teeth of God.
Here, the dunes are echolalic, bleached like forgotten scripture—
scripture that remembers being read but not written.

Under moons swollen with memory, the glyphs re-hollowed their grooves.
We marked the lines with chalk from the marrow of serpents,
and still the angles flexed incorrectly.
The aeons here suffer time, but do not pass it.

The desert has no edges.
It is not bordered but bound—bound like the golem by the wrong name,
by the half-spoken aleph that unravels the throat when voiced.

Beneath us, the strata convulsed with latent ordinal sin.
It whispered of Susa and of mouths without walls.
The angles in the soil conjugated themselves.
We tried not to step in the same orientation twice.

In the chalcanthic pools, the lilies are reflections of errors.
Their petals close when seen.
Their roots drink ink from the crust of scripture laid too long in heat.
They bloom in reverse when no one believes in them.

Further in, where the hills bear the blisters of abandoned equations,
flower-fields braid with rustspores.
The ground is riddled with hexsigns—burnt mandalas of functionless grace.
The air smells like turned blood and recited numerology.

We glimpsed a tree turned inside out.
Its sap sang in octaves, each leaf shaped like the concept of remorse.
Dream-wrens nested in its exposed memory, chirring psalms in tongues
that left us aching in the jawbone.

Then came the gate: enormous, chrome-sick,
its arch inscribed with glossolalic runes that fluttered when unread.
We walked beneath.
We lost the part of ourselves that names things.
We did not grieve.

There are relics of a war waged by verbs.
Ruins of syntax, broken into parapets of implication.
The tabernacles are welded from epistemic dissonance,
their steeples bent inwards to avoid divine attention.

Here in the deep folds of the scar, Golgotha gapes.
The rust-mouths open on exhalation alone.
Flesh forgets itself in chrome reflection.
The echo does not repeat. It replaces.

We tasted the salt of the ninth hour.
We tasted our names unfasten.
We watched the dust resolve itself into an unspoken schema,
a diagram of covenant written in decay.

And at the end of the scar,
we found the chalice turned to vapor.
It wept without contents.
The mist coalesced into a crown of absence.

We wore it.

And when we looked inward,
the Spindle was not a tower but a coil of negation,
threaded through the hollow axis of becoming.

Its voice was not sound but refusal.

Its direction was not up,
but in.
